CSS Slider

Super basic, for serious.

Alright, for starts, you might notice it doesn't slide. You might also notice that it doesn't go automatically like you would expect a JS slideshow to do. I'm working on both of these ideas, but right now I just wanted to get it out of my head and onto a webpage.

Some things to note

  1. This is really not a production ready idea. I was messing around with an image slider today and wondered if anything like this was possible.
  2. Yeah, I'm sure this has already been done; better, cleaner and more functional. I don't care, it was fun.
  3. For those who are wondering, the slider relies on a browser that recognizes and matches the :target psuedo selector to your URI hash. In your CSS you can make selectors that look like: .fu:target {
      /* Style stuff Goes Here */
    }     And then in your HTML you can have: <span class="fu" id="#bar">Hi, everybody!</span>
    <a href="#bar">Click Me</a>     And when you obey the “Click Me” imperative, it will apply your styles to id="#bar" (this will also work if you were to navigate to the URI manually like http://test.com#bar). See what I'm saying?
  4. When you click one of the controls, or load up a hashed URI, it's gonna bounce you right down to the slider. This may or may not be a good thing, but it's the thing you have. I don't know if there's any way to defeat it, even if you are using JavaScript.
